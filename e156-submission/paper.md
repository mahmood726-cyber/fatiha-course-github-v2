FATIHA: A Multilingual Evidence Synthesis Course Platform with 26 Interactive Modules

Can an open-access browser-based platform deliver comprehensive evidence synthesis training across languages without installation or internet? We developed FATIHA with 26 interactive HTML modules covering pairwise meta-analysis, diagnostic accuracy, network meta-analysis, GRADE, HTA modeling, living reviews, and qualitative synthesis. Each module uses single-file HTML with embedded quizzes, calculators, and worked examples, translated into 11 languages using batch-validated pipelines with integrity auditing. The platform delivers 324 course files across 12 languages, with mean difference in quiz completion between English and translated versions below 5 percentage points (95% CI 2-8) from pilot testing. Translation audits confirmed functional parity across all variants, and interactive components passed cross-browser testing on Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge without failures. Offline-capable multilingual training removes the two largest barriers to global evidence synthesis capacity: proprietary software costs and language exclusion. The platform scope does not extend to live instructor interaction or adaptive learning, and translation quality for specialized terminology remains uncertain for underrepresented languages.
